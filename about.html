About

Background, tools, and how I approach documentation work.

Background

I've spent over a decade writing and structuring technical documentation for industrial and enterprise software. My path started in the classroom — I taught for several years before moving into technical communication — and that instructional instinct still shapes how I write: I think about what a reader needs to know at the exact moment they need it, not just what's technically true.

Most recently, I owned knowledge management for a SaaS ERP platform end to end: over 20 modules, a weekly release cadence, and full administration of the ClickHelp CMS, including a style guide I built from scratch and the cross-functional review workflows that kept product, development, and QA aligned with what shipped in the docs.

Before that, at Epiroc Drilling Solutions, I worked as a Technical Editor and then Senior Technical Writer on structured authoring — DITA/XML content in XMetaL, technical editing in Adobe FrameMaker, and a migration of legacy documentation into a modern DITA-based CMS.

How I Work

I treat documentation as infrastructure, not an afterthought. That means writing content that's easy to find, easy to keep current, and structured so that one change doesn't require rewriting five pages. I'm comfortable being the sole owner of a knowledge base and building the process around it — style guides, review workflows, and templates — rather than waiting for that structure to be handed to me.

Outside of software documentation, I teach a research-writing course through a local homeschool co-op, which has kept my instructional design and curriculum-structuring skills active alongside my technical writing work.
