Concept

Feature Overview: Vendor Payment Batching

Vendor Payment Batching lets accounts payable teams group approved invoices into a single scheduled payment run, rather than releasing payments one at a time. A batch can combine invoices across vendors, payment methods, and due dates, and it respects any approval thresholds configured for the company.

Use this feature when your team processes a high volume of vendor invoices on a recurring schedule — for example, a weekly Friday payment run — and wants a single point of review before funds are released.

Task

Configure Batch Approval Thresholds

Before your team can use batching, an administrator sets the dollar threshold that determines whether a batch requires a second approver.

  1. Go to Settings > Payments > Approval Rules.
  2. Select Add Threshold Rule.
  3. Enter the dollar amount above which a batch requires a second approval.
  4. Choose whether the rule applies to all vendors or a specific vendor group.
  5. Select Save. The rule takes effect on the next batch created after saving; it does not apply retroactively to open batches.
Task

Create and Release a Payment Batch

  1. Go to Payments > Batches and select New Batch.
  2. Filter invoices by due date, vendor, or payment method, then select the invoices to include.
  3. Select Review Batch to see the total amount and invoice count before proceeding.
  4. Select Submit for Approval. If the batch total exceeds your configured threshold, it routes to a second approver automatically.
  5. Once approved, select Release Batch to send the batch to your payment processor.
A released batch cannot be edited. To remove an invoice after release, void the individual payment from Payments > History instead.
Troubleshooting

Batch Stuck in "Pending Approval"

CauseResolution
Assigned approver is inactive or has left the approval groupGo to Settings > Payments > Approval Rules and reassign the approver group, then resubmit the batch.
Batch total was edited after initial submissionAny change to batch total resets approval status. Resubmit the batch to restart the approval cycle.
Approver hasn't received notificationConfirm the approver's notification preferences under their user profile; batches don't resend notifications automatically.
Release Notes

Release Notes — Vendor Payment Batching

New Features & Updates

VersionChange
4.12Added support for mixed payment methods — ACH, check, and wire — within a single batch, instead of requiring a separate batch per payment method. Why it matters: AP teams no longer split one payment run into multiple batches just to accommodate different vendor payment preferences, which cuts batch-creation time on high-volume days.
4.9Approval threshold rules can now target a specific vendor group instead of applying one threshold company-wide. Why it matters: Finance can require tighter approval on higher-risk vendor groups without adding unnecessary review steps to routine, low-risk payments.

Localization

Writing Translation-Ready Source Content

Good localization starts before a translator ever sees the content. Writing choices made in the source English determine how cleanly a document translates, how much a translation vendor needs to guess at intent, and whether the same concept ends up with two different names in another language.

PracticeExampleWhy It Matters
One approved term per conceptAlways "batch," never a mix of "batch" and "group" for the same featurePrevents a translator from reasonably creating two different translated terms for what should be a single concept.
Mark non-translatable stringsProduct names and UI element labels flagged as do-not-translateKeeps brand names and interface labels consistent across every language version instead of being translated inconsistently by different vendors.
Avoid idioms and culturally specific phrasingWrite "resolve the issue" instead of "get to the bottom of it"Idioms rarely translate literally and can confuse or mislead a reader in another language.
Write shorter, simpler sentencesOne action per step instead of compound sentencesTranslated text commonly runs 20 to 30 percent longer than the English source; shorter source sentences help prevent translated UI text from overflowing.
Flag space-constrained UI textNote character limits next to any string tied to a button or labelPrevents a translated label from being truncated or breaking the layout once it's longer than the original English.

I owned these standards for the source content and partnered with translation vendors who handled the linguistic work itself, so localization stayed accurate and consistent release after release.
